About

I am an AI and NLP researcher interested in how language models represent, shape, and sometimes distort our understanding of the world. Methodologically, my work covers large language models, multilingual NLP, and evaluation frameworks that go beyond average accuracy: calibration, robustness, and failure analysis.

A recurring thread in my work is application in high-stakes contexts: monitoring risks to civilians, supporting humanitarian decision-making, and informing institutions that operate under uncertainty. I care about building systems that are technically solid, legible to practitioners, and grounded in human rights principles.

I’m particularly interested in:

  • Evaluation and analysis of large language models
  • Multilingual and low-resource NLP
  • Human-centered, responsible AI design
  • Interfaces between AI research, policy, and practice

Research

Broadly, I study how AI and NLP models behave across languages, domains, and stakes — and what it takes to make them reliable enough for people who depend on them.

Large Language Models & Evaluation

Building evaluation setups for LLMs that capture not only correctness, but also calibration, stability across prompts, and behavior under distribution shifts. This includes high-level metrics, error taxonomies, and qualitative analysis of model reasoning.

Multilingual & Low-Resource NLP

Studying how models perform in languages and domains that are under-represented in training data, and developing strategies for transfer, adaptation, and robustness in those settings.

Applied NLP for Humanitarian & Governance Use Cases

Designing NLP pipelines for open-source information monitoring, summarization, and risk signals that can support humanitarian actors, human rights defenders, and policy institutions — with an emphasis on traceability and uncertainty.

Data & Modeling for Social Systems

Working with news, event, and social media data to model social and political dynamics, including forecasting and early warning components, while being explicit about the limits of what models can see.

Teaching

I enjoy teaching and mentoring at the intersection of computer science, data science, and real-world impact. In my classes and supervision, I try to combine solid technical foundations with an honest look at where and how AI systems should (and should not) be used.

  • Big Data & Data Science – project-based learning using real-world datasets, with an emphasis on reproducibility, critical evaluation, and storytelling with data.
  • Senior Design / Capstone Projects – mentoring student teams on AI projects, often with social-impact themes and external stakeholders.
  • Introduction to Artificial Intelligence – foundations of AI alongside discussion of limitations, failure modes, and responsible deployment.
