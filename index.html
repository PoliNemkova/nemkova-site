About

I am an AI researcher working on natural language processing for humanitarian action, conflict forecasting, and human rights. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, public policy, and global governance, with a focus on making AI systems useful and safe in high-stakes, low-resource settings.

I care deeply about:

  • Human rights and protection of civilians
  • Multilingual and low-resource NLP
  • Responsible and transparent AI systems
  • Bridging research, policy, and practice

Over time, my goal is to help shape institutions, tools, and norms that make AI a stabilizing force rather than a source of new risks.

Research

My research focuses on NLP methods for high-stakes, real-world applications in conflict, human rights, and governance.

Human Rights & Atrocity Monitoring

Detecting and classifying references to human rights violations and attacks on civilians in social media and open-source text, with a focus on under-resourced and politically sensitive languages.

Conflict Forecasting & Crisis Analytics

Using news, social media, and event data to support early warning for conflict escalation, protests, and political violence, combining statistical models, NLP features, and large language models.

Multilingual & Low-Resource NLP

Evaluating and adapting language models for settings where data is scarce, noisy, or politically contested, including cross-lingual transfer and robustness for under-represented communities.

Evaluation & Governance of AI Systems

Designing evaluation setups for large language models in socially sensitive domains, including calibration, error analysis, and alignment with humanitarian principles and governance norms.

For a full list of ongoing and past projects, see Publications and Thoughts.

NGO & Impact

I am in the process of founding a nonprofit initiative that helps humanitarian organizations and social-impact institutions use AI responsibly and effectively.

The focus areas include:

  • Crisis monitoring and early-warning dashboards
  • Human rights and protection-of-civilians monitoring
  • Capacity building for NGOs on AI tools and data workflows
  • Guidance on responsible and safe AI use in the field

The goal is to make advanced AI methods accessible to practitioners working on peace, resilience, and human rights — without compromising ethics, safety, or local context.

A formal launch and more information will follow. If you are interested in collaborating or supporting this work, please reach out via Contact or Work with me.

Teaching

I enjoy teaching and mentoring at the intersection of computer science, data science, and real-world impact.

  • Big Data & Data Science – project-based learning using real-world datasets, with an emphasis on reproducibility and critical thinking.
  • Senior Design / Capstone Projects – mentoring student teams on AI projects, often with social-impact themes and real stakeholders.
  • Introduction to Artificial Intelligence – foundations of AI alongside discussion of responsible deployment and societal implications.

My teaching philosophy emphasizes curiosity, hands-on experimentation, and an honest examination of both the power and the limitations of AI systems.
